# StandUp

**StandUp** is a free Windows wellness app that reminds users to stand, stretch, and walk after long sitting sessions.

It is built for developers, gamers, students, remote workers, office users, and families who want a simple, privacy-friendly way to reduce uninterrupted screen time.

## Why StandUp?

Many people sit for long periods while working, studying, coding, gaming, or watching content. StandUp helps interrupt those long sitting sessions with configurable movement breaks.

The app can show a fullscreen reminder, start a short countdown, and encourage the user to stand up, stretch, and walk before returning to work or play.

## Key Features

* Configurable sitting interval: 30, 45, 60 minutes, or custom
* Configurable break duration: 2, 3, 5 minutes, or custom
* Fullscreen movement reminder
* Multi-monitor support
* System tray app
* Parent/Admin mode
* Password-protected settings
* Optional start with Windows
* Works offline
* No account required
* No cloud dependency
* No tracking

## Use Cases

### Personal Wellness Mode

For adults, developers, office workers, and remote professionals who want gentle but firm movement reminders.

### Family / Parent Mode

For parents who want to help children or teenagers take healthy breaks during gaming or long screen sessions.

In Family Mode, the child can complete the break and return automatically, while only the parent/admin can change settings, pause the app, or exit it.

## Privacy

StandUp is designed to be local-first.

The app does not require an account, does not upload personal data, does not record the screen, does not use the camera, and does not send usage data to any server.

## Health & Safety Disclaimer

StandUp is a wellness reminder tool. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or a medical device.

If you have symptoms such as one-sided leg swelling or pain, warmth or redness in the leg, chest pain, sudden shortness of breath, fainting, or coughing blood, seek urgent medical help immediately.

People with medical conditions, clotting risk factors, or concerns about deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, or prolonged sitting should consult a qualified healthcare professional.
